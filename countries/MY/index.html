Malaysia’s history includes early Malay kingdoms, colonial rule by the Portuguese, Dutch, and British, and occupation by Japan during World War II. It gained independence in 1957 and later merged with several territories to form modern Malaysia. Today, it is a rapidly developing nation with a strong economy based on manufacturing, trade, and tourism.

Malaysia is a Southeast Asian country consisting of two regions: Peninsular Malaysia, which shares borders with Thailand to the north and Singapore to the south, and East Malaysia, located on the island of Borneo, sharing borders with Indonesia and Brunei. Covering a total area of approximately 330,803 square kilometers, Malaysia has a population of about 32 million people as of 2023. The capital city is Kuala Lumpur, while Putrajaya serves as the administrative center. The official language is Malay. Malaysia operates as a federal constitutional monarchy, with a king serving as the head of state and a prime minister as the head of government. The economy is diverse and robust, with key sectors including manufacturing, services, and agriculture. Malaysia is known for its cultural diversity, tropical rainforests, and landmarks such as the Petronas Twin Towers. The country is a member of international organizations such as the United Nations, the Association of Southeast Asian Nations (ASEAN), and the Commonwealth of Nations.
