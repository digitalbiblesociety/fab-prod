Myanmar, formerly Burma, has a history of powerful kingdoms, including the Pagan and Konbaung dynasties. It was a British colony from 1885 until independence in 1948. After decades of military rule, democratic reforms began in the 2010s, though political instability and ethnic conflicts persist. The country remains rich in resources but faces economic challenges.

Myanmar, officially the Republic of the Union of Myanmar, is a country in Southeast Asia. It shares borders with Bangladesh and India to the northwest, China to the northeast, Laos and Thailand to the east and southeast, and has a coastline along the Andaman Sea and the Bay of Bengal to the south and southwest. Covering an area of approximately 676,578 square kilometers, Myanmar has a population of about 54 million people as of 2023. The capital city is Naypyidaw, while the largest city is Yangon (formerly Rangoon). The official language is Burmese. Myanmar operates as a unitary parliamentary republic. The economy is primarily based on agriculture, mining, and energy, with significant natural resources including oil, natural gas, and minerals. Myanmar is known for its rich cultural heritage, ancient temples, and diverse ethnic groups. The country is a member of international organizations such as the United Nations, the Association of Southeast Asian Nations (ASEAN), and the World Trade Organization.
