Indonesia’s history includes early Hindu-Buddhist kingdoms such as Srivijaya and Majapahit before the arrival of Islam in the 13th century. The Dutch colonized the archipelago in the 17th century, ruling until independence was declared in 1945. Indonesia has since grown into Southeast Asia’s largest economy, known for its cultural diversity and strategic location as the world's largest island nation.

Indonesia, officially the Republic of Indonesia, is a Southeast Asian nation comprising over 17,000 islands, including Sumatra, Java, Borneo (shared with Malaysia and Brunei), Sulawesi, and New Guinea (shared with Papua New Guinea). It is the world's largest archipelagic state, covering approximately 1.904 million square kilometers, and has a population of around 273 million people as of 2023, making it the fourth-most populous country. The capital city is Jakarta, located on the island of Java. The official language is Indonesian (Bahasa Indonesia). Indonesia operates as a presidential republic. The economy is diverse, with key sectors including agriculture, manufacturing, mining, and services. Indonesia is known for its cultural diversity, with hundreds of ethnic groups and languages, and is a member of international organizations such as the United Nations, the Association of Southeast Asian Nations (ASEAN), and the G20.
