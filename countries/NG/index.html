Nigeria was home to advanced pre-colonial civilizations such as the Nok, Hausa, Yoruba, and Igbo states. It became a British colony in the 19th century and gained independence in 1960. Since then, Nigeria has experienced military rule, civil war, and democratic transitions. It is Africa’s largest economy, driven by oil, agriculture, and a growing tech industry.

Nigeria, officially the Federal Republic of Nigeria, is a country in West Africa. It is bordered by Niger to the north, Chad to the northeast, Cameroon to the east, and Benin to the west, with a southern coastline along the Gulf of Guinea in the Atlantic Ocean. Covering an area of approximately 923,769 square kilometers, Nigeria has a population of over 200 million people as of 2023, making it the most populous country in Africa. The capital is Abuja, while the largest city is Lagos. The official language is English, with over 500 indigenous languages spoken across the country. Nigeria operates as a federal presidential republic. The economy is diverse, with key sectors including oil and gas, agriculture, telecommunications, and services. Nigeria is known for its rich cultural heritage, diverse ethnic groups, and as a leading producer of oil. The country is a member of international organizations such as the United Nations, the African Union, and the Economic Community of West African States.
