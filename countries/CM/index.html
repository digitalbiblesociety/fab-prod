Cameroon was home to various indigenous kingdoms before European contact. It became a German colony in the late 19th century and was later divided between France and Britain after World War I. Gaining independence in 1960, the country merged its French and British territories into one republic. Cameroon has since faced political tensions but remains a key economic hub in Central Africa, known for its cultural diversity and natural resources.

Cameroon, officially the Republic of Cameroon, is a country in Central Africa. It is bordered by Nigeria to the west and north, Chad to the northeast, the Central African Republic to the east, and Equatorial Guinea, Gabon, and the Republic of the Congo to the south. The country also has a coastline along the Bight of Biafra. Covering an area of approximately 475,442 square kilometers, Cameroon has a population of over 25 million people. The capital is Yaoundé, while the largest city and economic hub is Douala. The official languages are French and English. Cameroon operates as a unitary dominant-party presidential republic. The economy is diverse, with key sectors including agriculture, mining, oil and gas, and services. Cameroon is a member of the African Union, the United Nations, and the Commonwealth of Nations.
