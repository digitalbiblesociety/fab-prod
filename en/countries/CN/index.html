Languages:

China, officially the People's Republic of China, is a vast country in East Asia with a diverse linguistic landscape. The official language is Mandarin, or Standard Chinese, which is based on the Beijing dialect and spoken by approximately 70% of the population as of 2021. China is home to more than 290 living languages, including major regional languages such as Cantonese, Shanghainese, and Hokkien, as well as various ethnic minority languages like Tibetan, Uighur, and Zhuang. While Mandarin is widely spoken and promoted by the government for national unity, many people in regional and ethnic communities continue to use their native languages for daily communication.
